Admissions

Start Your Journey With Us

We welcome motivated students from around the world who are passionate about engineering and innovation.

Why Apply to Dzhusik?

Joining Dzhusik Engineering College means becoming part of a vibrant community dedicated to excellence, innovation, and making a positive impact on the world. Our rigorous yet supportive academic environment prepares students for successful careers in engineering and technology.

We seek students who demonstrate academic excellence, creativity, leadership potential, and a strong commitment to their chosen field of study.

Application Process

Follow these steps to submit your application

Step 1: Complete Online Application

Fill out the registration form with your personal information, academic history, and program preferences. Make sure all information is accurate and complete.

Step 2: Submit Required Documents

Upload your academic transcripts, standardized test scores, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement outlining your interest in engineering.

Step 3: Application Review

Our admissions committee carefully reviews each application holistically, considering academic performance, test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal qualities.

Step 4: Interview (If Required)

Some applicants may be invited for an interview with faculty members or admissions officers to discuss their goals and interests further.

Step 5: Receive Decision

Admission decisions are typically communicated within four weeks of completing your application. Accepted students will receive enrollment instructions and scholarship information.

Step 6: Confirm Enrollment

Accept your offer of admission by the specified deadline and complete the enrollment process, including payment of the enrollment deposit.

Admission Requirements

What you need to apply

  • High school diploma or equivalent certificate
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale
  • SAT scores (minimum 1200) or ACT scores (minimum 25)
  • English proficiency test (TOEFL 80+ or IELTS 6.5+) for international students
  • Two letters of recommendation from teachers or mentors
  • Personal statement explaining your interest in engineering
  • Official transcripts from all secondary schools attended

  • Bachelor's degree in engineering or related field from an accredited institution
  • Minimum undergraduate GPA of 3.2 on a 4.0 scale
  • GRE scores (recommended minimum: Verbal 150, Quantitative 160)
  • English proficiency test (TOEFL 90+ or IELTS 7.0+) for international students
  • Three letters of recommendation from professors or professional supervisors
  • Statement of purpose outlining research interests and career goals
  • Resume or curriculum vitae
  • Official transcripts from all universities attended

  • All standard admission requirements listed above
  • Credential evaluation for foreign transcripts
  • Proof of financial support for tuition and living expenses
  • Valid passport
  • Student visa documentation
  • Health insurance coverage
